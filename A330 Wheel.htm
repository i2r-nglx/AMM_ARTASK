Removal of the MLG Wheel
WARNING:
MAKE SURE THAT THE SAFETY DEVICES AND THE WARNING NOTICES ARE IN POSITION BEFORE YOU START A TASK ON OR NEAR:
-
THE FLIGHT CONTROLS
-
THE FLIGHT CONTROL SURFACES
-
THE LANDING GEAR AND THE RELATED DOORS
-
COMPONENTS THAT MOVE.
MOVEMENT OF COMPONENTS CAN KILL OR CAUSE INJURY TO PERSONS AND/OR CAN CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE EQUIPMENT.
WARNING:
LET THE BRAKES AND THE WHEELS BECOME COOL BEFORE YOU GO NEAR THE LANDING GEAR. DO NOT APPLY A LIQUID OR GAS FIRE EXTINGUISHER DIRECTLY ON A HOT WHEEL OR BRAKE UNIT. THIS COULD CAUSE AN EXPLOSION.
WARNING:
BE CAREFUL WHEN YOU USE CONSUMABLE MATERIALS. OBEY THE MATERIAL MANUFACTURER'S INSTRUCTIONS AND YOUR LOCAL REGULATIONS.

Subtask 32-41-11-020-050-B
WARNING:
DECREASE THE TIRE PRESSURE TO APPROXIMATELY 2 BAR (29 PSI) BEFORE YOU REMOVE THE WHEEL. THIS WILL PREVENT A POSSIBLE EXPLOSION.
A.
Removal of the MLG Wheel
NOTE:
When you remove the wheel for access, it is not necessary to decrease the tire pressure when these conditions are applicable:
·
The wheel and tire are serviceable
·
The tire is at its correct pressure
·
A label is put on the wheel that specifies the tire pressure.
(1)
Decrease the tire pressure to 2.0 bar (29.0075 psi).
(a)
Remove the cap (7) from the inflation valve.
(b)
Attach an ADAPTER - TIRE INFLATION/DEFLATION to the inflation valve.
(c)
Decrease the tire pressure until the pressure gage shows 2.0 bar (29.0075 psi).
(d)
Remove the tire inflation adaptor.
(e)
Put the cap (7) on the inflation valve.
(2)
Put the   TOOL-R I WHEELS  (98F32401001000)  or STANDARD WHEEL R AND I DOLLY (98F32401001000 TYPE) below the wheel.
(3)
Remove and discard the cotter pins (6) and the nuts (5).
(4)
Remove the washers (4) and (8) and the bolts (2).
(5)
Use the   SPANNER-AXLE NUT, MLG  (460007011)  and remove the axle nut (3).
(6)
Install the   GUIDE CONE-WHEEL  (M47381)  .
(7)
Remove the wheel (1) with the applicable tool.
(8)
If necessary, clean the applicable MLG axle-equipment carrier as follows:
(a)
Remove the axle equipment carrier  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-48-52-000-801)  .
(b)
Use a Textile-Lint free Cotton -  (Material No.  14SBA1 ) and Non Aqueous Cleaner-General - -  (Material No.  08BAA9 ) to clean the component interfaces.
(c)
Lubricate and install the axle equipment carrier  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-48-52-400-801)  .
NOTE:
It is recommended to clean the axle equipment carrier at each wheel change to remove carbon brake dust. This will help in easy removal of the axle equipment carrier at the next maintenance check.
(9)
Put a COVER - PROTECTION on the axle assembly.
(10)
Do an inspection of the brake stator slots:
(a)
For Goodrich-Messier brakes refer to SB 32-3238 or SB 32-4280.
(Ref. Fig. Goodrich Brakes - Inspection of the MLG Brake Stator Slots for Enlargement - Flow Chart) 
(b)
For Honeywell brakes refer to SB 32-3251 or SB 32-4288.
(Ref. Fig. Honeywell Brakes - Inspection of the MLG Brake Stator Slots for Enlargement - Flow Chart)

C.
Preparation for Installation
(1)
Remove the COVER - PROTECTION from the axle assembly.
(2)
Make sure that the parts retained from the removed component are clean and in the correct condition.
(3)
Clean the component interface and/or the adjacent area.
(4)
Remove all the brake dust particles.
(5)
Do a visual inspection of the component interface and/or the adjacent area.
(6)
Do an inspection of the tachometer drive shaft assembly.
Make sure that the tachometer drive shaft is:
·
Not bent or distorted
·
Free of fretting marks
·
Not scratched, stained or corroded
·
In a serviceable condition and the splines are not damaged
·
Free to turn.
(7)
If the tachometer drive is worn or damaged, you must replace it  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-42-57-000-801)  and  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-42-57-400-801)  .
(8)
Do an inspection to make sure that the axle sleeve spring clip is serviceable and correctly installed  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-41-14-400-801)  .
(9)
If applicable, do an inspection of the brake stator slots:
(a)
For Goodrich-Messier brakes refer to SB 32-3238.
(Ref. Fig. Goodrich Brakes - Inspection of the MLG Brake Stator Slots for Enlargement - Flow Chart) 
(b)
For Honeywell brakes refer to SB 32-3251.
(Ref. Fig. Honeywell Brakes - Inspection of the MLG Brake Stator Slots for Enlargement - Flow Chart) 
(10)
If applicable, do an inspection of the brake wear indicator  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-40-00-210-802)  .
(11)
Examine the brake for hydraulic leaks from the hose connections and the pistons.
(12)
Examine the brake heat pack. Make sure that:
·
There is no hydraulic fluid contamination of the carbon parts
·
There are no broken rotors or stators
·
There is no wear in the rotor drive slots
·
There is no foreign object damage
·
There are no holes, cracks or chips
·
There are no soft areas, where carbon is missing or crushed
·
There are no broken or missing links
·
There are no broken or missing rivets and the rivet holes are not worn
·
There is no oxidation
·
There is no other possible damage.
(13)
If the brake is unserviceable, you must replace it  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-42-27-000-801)  and  (Ref. AMM TASK 32-42-27-400-801)  .
(14)
If there is a fault in the brake disk:
·
Record the applicable data in the brake-disk failure report-sheet and send to AIRBUS.
(Ref. Fig. Brake-Disk Failure Report-Sheet) 
(15)
Do an inspection of the heat shield in the wheel to make sure that it is serviceable.
(16)
Make sure that there is no damage to the component interface and the adjacent area.
(17)
If the brake rotors are not aligned, do these steps:
(a)
On panel 117VU, put the PARK BRK lever to the OFF position.
(b)
Align the brake rotors with the brake stators.
(c)
On panel 117VU, put the PARK BRK lever to the ON position.
(18)
Lubricate the axle sleeve:
WARNING:
YOU MUST ONLY APPLY A VERY SMALL QUANTITY OF GREASE. A VERY HOT BRAKE CAN CAUSE THE GREASE TO BURN AND CAUSE A FIRE.
(a)
Put a very small amount of Synthetic Oil base Grease-General Purpose Clay Thickened -  (Material No.  03GBB1 ) on the axle sleeve, only where the wheel bearings will touch when the wheel is installed.
(b)
Use a clean Textile-Lint free Cotton -  (Material No.  14SBA1 ) to remove unwanted grease from the axle sleeve.

Subtask 32-41-11-420-050-B
A.
Installation of the MLG Wheel
(1)
Make sure that the   GUIDE CONE-WHEEL  (M47381)  is installed correctly.
(2)
Use the   TOOL-R I WHEELS  (98F32401001000)  or STANDARD WHEEL R AND I DOLLY (98F32401001000 TYPE) to align the wheel (1) with the brake rotors.
(3)
Push the wheel (1) fully on the axle.
(4)
Remove the   GUIDE CONE-WHEEL  (M47381)  .
(5)
Examine the tachometer drive assembly (as much as possible) on the wheel (1).
(6)
Make sure that the drive shaft assembly and splines were not damaged during the wheel (1) installation.
(7)
Apply a thin layer of Grease-Thread Anti-seize Graphited and Petrolatum -  (Material No.  03LBB1 ) to the splines of the tachometer drive shaft.
(8)
Apply a layer of Synthetic Oil base Grease-General Purpose Clay Thickened -  (Material No.  03GBB1 ) to the MLG axle thread.
(9)
Install the axle nut (3) on the axle and use the   SPANNER-AXLE NUT, MLG  (460007011)  to tighten it.
(10)
On panel 117VU, put the PARK BRK lever to the OFF position.
(11)
TORQUE the axle nut (3)  to 54.2 m.daN  (399.71 lbf.ft) while you slowly turn the wheel in the same direction.
(12)
Loosen the axle nut (3) fully while you turn the wheel slowly in the same direction.
(13)
TORQUE the axle nut (3)  to 30.5 m.daN  (224.93 lbf.ft) while you slowly turn the wheel in the same direction.
(14)
If necessary, tighten the axle nut (3) until two of its slots align with the bolt holes in the axle.
(15)
Do not tighten to more than 44.0 m.daN (324.48 lbf.ft).
(16)
Make sure that the holes in the axle equipment carrier are also aligned.
(17)
Install the bolts (2) with the bolt heads on the outer surface of the axle.
(18)
Install the  IPC -CSN ( 32-11-14-20-075 ) washer (8) ,  IPC -CSN ( 32-11-14-20-073 ) washers (4) and the new  IPC -CSN ( 32-11-14-20-060 ) nuts (5).
(19)
TORQUE the nuts (5)  to between 0.4 and 0.45 m.daN  (35.40 and 39.82 lbf.in) .
(20)
Safety the nuts (5) with the new  IPC -CSN ( 32-11-14-20-050 ) cotter pins (6) . Make sure that the cotter pins are bent radially around the nuts.
(21)
Turn the wheel (1) to make sure that it is installed correctly.
